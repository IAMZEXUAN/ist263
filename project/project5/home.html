What is an arowana fish?

Arowana is a type of large freshwater fish that belongs to the Osteoglossidae family, which originated as early as the Cretaceous period (145-66 million years ago). Two of the most commonly known species of the arowana family are the Osteoglossum bicirrhosum, aka the silver arowana, and the Scleropages formosus, aka the asian arowana. These two species are also the more popular ones in aquariums, especially the asian arowana, which is highly valued for its appearance and cultural significance. While they are both called arowana, the two species are in fact very distinct from each other in terms of their habitats, body shape, etc. And those differences also lead to different care needs.

Depending on the type of fish that interests you, please refer to our guidelines accordingly.
